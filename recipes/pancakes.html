Pancakes

This is my favorite basic pancakes recipe. The final product has a great vanilla flavor and a bit of sweetness. Feel free to increase the ratios and make a lot more pancakes. They freeze well to be eaten another day!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp (14g) butter
  • 2 tbsp (25g) sugar
  • 1 egg (just use the yolk if you're making a half-batch)
  • 2 tsp (5 ml) vanilla
  • 1 3/4 cups (400 ml) milk or buttermilk
  • 1 tsp (6g) salt
  • 1 heaped tbsp (16g) baking powder (or 1 tsp [6g] baking soda if using buttermilk)
  • 1.5-2.5 cups (200-300g) all-purpose flour
  • Additional butter for the pan

Steps

  1. Melt the butter without getting it too hot. I usually microwave it for about 30 seconds or so and stir it until melted. You don't want it hot or the egg will cook from the heat.

  2. Mix in the sugar.

  3. Beat the egg in thoroughly

  4. Mix in the vanilla, milk/buttermilk, salt and baking powder/soda. Mix well and know that once you add flour you must be careful not to over-mix.

  5. Start mixing in a conservative amount of flour, then keep adding until you get the thickness you want. The thicker the batter, the thicker the pancake. Be sure to not over-mix â€” the batter should be full of small lumps.

  6. Let the batter rest a few minutes while you heat up your pan.

  7. Heat your cooking surface of choice (I use nonstick) to a low-moderate heat and smear it with butter. When the butter is just starting to brown, pour on the pancakes.

  8. When there are lots of bubbles on the surface, flip the pancakes and continue cooking until they look cooked, then cook another minute before removing to a cooling rack.
